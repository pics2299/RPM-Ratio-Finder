To get the ratio you want, you need Adjustable Chain Gearshifts - called "ACGs" in the table - and sets of large and small cogwheels to double or halve the rotation speed, called "gear changes". The orange numbers in the ACG sequence are the redstone levels to give to the ACGs on the output of a chain and the blue numbers are the redstone levels on the input. The order in which the ACGs are placed doesn't matter, but it is good practice to put in first the steps that slow down the rotation, and then the steps that speed it up, to avoid going over the maximum speed (256 RPM by default). The number of gear changes refers to the number of times you need to double (on a green background) or halve (on a red background) the rotation speed in addition to the ACG sequence. If several lines are present for the same target, they all have the same precision and you can choose any of the solutions.
